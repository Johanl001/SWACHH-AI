## Welcome

This guide explains how to use the SWACHH-AI Worker App on your phone. The app tells you exactly **which bins to collect** and **the best route to drive** — so you spend less time on the road and collect more efficiently.

**Language:** This guide is available in English. हिंदी और मराठी versions are available in the app.

---

## 1. Setting Up Your Phone

### 1.1 Download the App

- **Android:** Search "SWACHH-AI Worker" on Google Play → Install
  *(The Citizen App and Worker App are separate apps)*

### 1.2 Login

1. Open the app
2. Enter your **Employee ID** and **Password** (given by your supervisor)
3. Tap **Login**
4. Allow **Location permission** when asked — this is required for navigation

> If you forgot your password, call your zone supervisor.

## 3. Starting Your Route

### Step 1: Check Today's Route

When your supervisor assigns a route, you will get a **notification on your phone.** Open the app — the route is ready on your home screen.

Tap **"TODAY'S ROUTE"** to see:
- List of bins in order
- Distance between each stop
- Expected time at each stop
- Total route summary

### Step 2: Start Navigation

1. Tap **▶ START ROUTE**
2. The app opens Google Maps (or built-in navigation) with the **first bin's location**
3. Follow the directions on screen
4. When you arrive at the bin, the app shows a **"ARRIVED"** notification

### Step 3: Collect the Bin

1. Empty the bin as usual
2. Come back to the app
3. Tap **"✅ Mark as Collected"**
4. The app automatically navigates you to the **next bin**

Repeat until all bins on the route are marked collected.

## 5. Bin Colour Guide

When you look at the map or bin list, bins are colour-coded:

| Colour | Fill Level | Priority |
|--------|-----------|----------|
| 🔴 Red | > 80% full | **Must collect today** — on your route |
| 🟡 Yellow | 60–80% full | Monitor — may be added if time allows |
| 🟢 Green | < 60% full | Skip — does not need collection today |
| ⚫ Grey | Offline / No signal | Report to supervisor |

> **Your route only includes 🔴 Red bins.** You do not need to visit green or yellow bins unless your supervisor says otherwise.

---

## 6. Reporting a Problem

If you find a problem at any bin, tap **🚩 Report Problem:**

| Problem | What to select |
|---------|---------------|
| Bin is damaged / broken | "Bin Damaged" |
| Bin is already empty (someone else collected) | "Already Empty" |
| Cannot access bin (blocked by vehicle) | "Access Blocked" |
| Area is unsafe / accident | "Safety Concern" |
| Bin sensor not working (no reading updating) | "Sensor Issue" |

After you report, your supervisor is notified immediately. You can skip that bin and continue your route.

---

## 7. Marking the Route Complete

When all bins are collected:

1. The app shows: **"All bins collected! ✅"**
2. Tap **"Complete Route"**
3. A summary is sent to your supervisor:
   - Bins collected: 8/8
   - Time taken: 44 min
   - Distance driven: 11.8 km
4. Your shift record is saved automatically

If you could not collect all bins (e.g., time ran out or access issues), tap **"End Route Early"** and select a reason. Remaining bins are flagged for the next shift.

## 8. Live Map

Tap **🗺️ View Map** to see all bins in your zone on a map:

- Your current location is shown as a **blue arrow**
- Your assigned bins are highlighted
- Tap any bin for details (address, fill level, last update)

Use this if you need to find a bin you're unfamiliar with.

   - Time on duty
3. Log out if you are sharing a device with another worker

## 11. Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: The app says I have 8 bins but my supervisor said 6. Which is correct?**
A: The app is updated in real time. If the number changed, it means 2 more bins crossed the critical level after the route was first made. Collect all bins shown in the app.

**Q: I can't find the bin at the address shown. What do I do?**
A: Tap the bin → **"Report Problem"** → **"Cannot Locate Bin."** Move to the next stop.

**Q: My phone battery is dying. Can I continue on paper?**
A: Yes — tap **"Route"** → **"Print / Share Route"** to send yourself an SMS with the bin list and addresses before your phone dies.

**Q: The navigation is taking me the wrong way.**
A: Use Google Maps directly if needed — the bin address is shown on screen. The optimized route in the app is a suggestion, not a mandatory path.

**Q: I collected a bin that wasn't on my route because it was overflowing.**
A: Tap **📋 All Bins** → find that bin → tap **"Mark as Collected (Unscheduled)."** This updates the system so no one else collects it again.

## 12. Tips for Efficient Collection

- **Start early** — bins fill faster in morning market hours
- **Check the map** before starting — mentally note where each bin is
- **Report sensor issues** immediately — broken sensors mean supervisors can't plan routes properly
- **Don't skip yellow bins** if you have time — they may turn red by tomorrow

---

## 13. Safety Reminders

- Always wear your **high-visibility vest** and **gloves** when handling bins
- Park the truck safely — do not block traffic while collecting
- Do not manually move broken bins without reporting first
- If a bin has hazardous waste (needles, chemicals), **do not touch** — report to supervisor immediately
